Amenities
Raunds Lodge Nursing Home will try to provide its service users with a secure, relaxed and homely environment in which their care, well being and comfort are of prime importance.
The management and staff will endeavour to maintain the individuality, independence, dignity and respect those in our care. We will ensure the staff and service users enjoy happy and homely atmosphere at all times.

The accommodation is arranged on two floors:

Downstairs:
There are 13 bedrooms, 11 single with en-suite and 2 double rooms. Ground floor accommodates a total of 15 service users.

Upstairs:
There are 14 bedrooms on the first floor, of which 12 are single and 2 are double. 11 rooms are en-suit. First floor accommodates a total of 16 service users.

Social Room:
There is one large lounge and diner in the existing building and another in the new building. Service users are encouraged to use these areas, however, Service Users who wish to stay in their own rooms may do so. Smoking is not permitted in the home. All rooms are connected to a nurse call system for the benefit and safety of service users.

ADMISSION
Service users interested in coming to Raunds Lodge Nursing Home are encouraged to visit the home and sample the atmosphere and level of service. A month trial period is always given before taking permanent residency. The home is registered with Commission for Social Care & Inspections to provide Nursing or Personal ( Residential ) care and accommodation for 31 persons including those suffering from dementia. The care plan will be based on person centred approach. This approach considers the impact of process of dementia and the individual’s identity, behaviour and emotional needs. Wherever possible the home will seek the support of external networks such as Medical Professionals, Community Psychiatric Nurse and friends and families of the resident. The home will ensure that every prospective resident has their needs assessed before a decision on admission is taken. It would not be the home’s aim to care for a resident who is presenting physically challenging and aggressive behaviour, which may cause other residents, staff or themselves to be in danger. Where a resident needs to be moved to a more appropriate unit, the home will work with the resident’s representatives and medical professionals to ensure that the process of the move is made with minimum of fuss and unease.
